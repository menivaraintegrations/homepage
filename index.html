I help businesses stop fighting their own tools.

Something in your business is taking longer than it should. You probably know what it is. I find the fix and build it—whether that's an automation, a better process, or just connecting things that should already be talking to each other.

let's talk

You know that spreadsheet someone updates every Friday? The one that takes two hours and nobody enjoys?

Or the three tools your team uses that don't talk to each other, so someone copies data between them manually?

Or the report your boss asks for monthly that requires pulling numbers from four different places?

That stuff doesn't have to be that way. Most businesses have operational friction they've just learned to live with. An outside set of eyes with the right toolkit can fix it faster than you think.

How I Can Help

I don't sell packages. I listen to what's broken and figure out the right fix. Here's the kind of stuff I work on:

🔍

Process Diagnosis

Where is time being wasted and why? Sometimes the answer isn't new software—it's using what you already have in a smarter way.

🔗

Tool Integration

Your CRM, calendar, invoicing, and email should talk to each other. I connect the tools you already use so data flows where it needs to go.

⚡

Workflow Automation

That thing you do every Monday morning for two hours? Let's make it take five minutes. I build automations that handle the repetitive stuff so your team doesn't have to.

📊

Data Organization

Your information exists but it's scattered across six spreadsheets and someone's email. I bring it together so you can actually use it.

🤖

AI Implementation

There are AI tools that can handle 80% of tasks you're doing manually. I'll show you which ones make sense for your business and set them up.

💡

Operational Clarity

You don't always need new software. Sometimes you need to stop using the software you have in the wrong way. I help you see the difference.

How It Works

No long proposals. No jargon. Just a straightforward path from "something's not working" to "that's fixed now."

1

Discovery

We have a conversation about what's frustrating you. No pitch, no proposal template—just listening. I'll identify the things that are costing you time or money. This one's free.

2

Quick Win

I scope a small, contained project—usually 1-2 weeks. Fixed price, clear outcome. You see results fast without committing to a huge engagement. This is where trust gets built.

3

Ongoing

If the quick win goes well and there's more to tackle, we can set up an ongoing relationship. Flexible scope, month to month. Some months heavy, some months light.

The Person Behind This

I'm Andrew. My career path has been... eclectic. Restaurant general manager. Marketing director. The guy who kept saying "there has to be a better way to do this" until he started actually building the better way.

I don't come from a software engineering background. I come from operations—from managing real teams, real margins, and real chaos. That means when I look at your business, I'm not thinking about what's technically elegant. I'm thinking about what actually saves you time and money.

I work with automation platforms, AI tools, and a healthy dose of common sense. The combination lets me solve problems that are too technical for most business consultants and too practical for most developers.

I'm honest about what I know and what I'm figuring out. If something's outside my wheelhouse, I'll tell you. If the answer is "you don't need me for this," I'll tell you that too.

Questions You Might Have

What kind of businesses do you work with?
+
Mostly small and mid-size businesses—5 to 50 employees. The kind where the owner or operations manager is the decision maker, not a procurement committee. I work across industries but have the most experience with manufacturing, food service, retail, trades, and professional services.
How much does this cost?
+
The discovery call is free. Quick win projects typically range from $500 to $2,500 depending on complexity. Larger projects and ongoing retainers are scoped individually. I price on value delivered, not hours worked—a two-hour fix that saves you ten hours a week is worth more than two hours of billing.
Do I need to be technical?
+
Not at all. If you can describe what's frustrating you in plain language, that's all I need. I handle the technical side. When I'm done, I'll make sure you and your team understand what was built and how to use it.
What if I'm not sure what the problem is?
+
That's fine—and honestly pretty common. You know something is inefficient but can't pinpoint exactly what. That's what the discovery conversation is for. I ask questions, you describe your day-to-day, and usually the problems become obvious pretty quickly.
What tools and platforms do you use?
+
I'm platform-flexible. I work with Make.com, n8n, Google Workspace, ClickUp, various AI tools, and whatever else fits the situation. I don't push any particular tool—I use what makes sense for your specific problem and what your team is already comfortable with.
